02 - Project work

Contributions

My contributions to the Fedora Project during the Outreachy 2026 contribution phase.

#124 - Exploring RamaLama: how it makes working with AI boring Completed

Installed RamaLama on Fedora 43 and explored how it pulls and runs AI models locally using different transports - Ollama, HuggingFace, and OCI registries. Tested four models: granite:2b, merlinite:7b, tinyllama, and gemma3:12b. Each had its own personality - tinyllama was fast but shallow, merlinite needed a --temp 0 flag to stop hallucinating, and gemma3:12b was so heavy it timed out on ramalama run. Fixed that by serving it as a server and querying via curl - something I had previously learned while studying multithreading in OS, which was a fun connection to make.

The biggest surprise was that RamaLama automatically detected my RTX 3050 via Vulkan - no CUDA needed. I only found out through the container logs. Also learned that OCI registries require a Red Hat account while Ollama and HuggingFace allow anonymous access.

05 - Reflection

What I enjoyed most

Honestly? The blogs. I did not expect to enjoy writing so much during a tech internship application. Writing the onboarding guide especially felt meaningful - knowing that someone going through the same confusion I had might read it and find it helpful. That is not nothing.

06 - Honest take

What challenged me

The documentation work - and it is still ongoing. Fixing docs sounds simple from the outside but it is really not. Every change needs to be fact-checked against what the tool actually does, formatted consistently with other pages, and coordinated with other contributors so nothing conflicts. It requires a lot of patience and attention to detail.

The RamaLama exploration also had its moments. I tried enabling CUDA acceleration but ran into kernel conflicts - no wifi on the new kernel after rebooting. Had to proceed with CPU only. And when gemma3:12b kept timing out on ramalama run, I had to dig into container logs with podman logs just to understand what was happening. That kind of debugging - where you don't know what you're looking for - is the part nobody tells you about.

07 - Something unexpected

What surprised me

My RTX 3050 getting detected automatically via Vulkan - without any CUDA setup. I only found out because I ran podman logs on a stuck container. RamaLama had already figured it out quietly in the background. That felt like a small magic trick.

Also the difference between model sizes was more dramatic than I expected. TinyLlama (0.46GB) responded in seconds but barely scratched the surface. Merlinite 7b (4.07GB) was slower but gave proper, detailed answers. And gemma3:12b (7.59GB) needed to be run as a server just to work at all on my machine. Seeing that tradeoff between size, speed, and quality in real time was genuinely interesting.
